Windy Weather Wonderland

Written: March 13, 2019   

         The Dutch weather leaves a little bit to be desired for. For the first month I was here, it’s been unseasonably nice weather. At my orientation the dean of students had joked that we probably wouldn’t see the sun until April at the earliest. I had written that comment off after all of the sun, but now I think I’m starting to understand what he meant. For example, the past week has been rainy, cold, and windy. Apparently, this is how the climate is normally supposed to be. Rain and cold, not a big deal, I’ve dealt with that before. What has been destroying me is the wind. I’ll have you know, it’s no because I’m ruined from the beautiful weather in Phoenix. Oh no, it all comes back to biking. As most of you know, I have been steadily improving my biking skills. I bike ever day to school and pretty much everywhere that I need to go. The only exception being the grocery store because I’m still too afraid to figure out how to carry all my bags while staying upright. I like to think that I’m starting to move out of the novice category and more into an intermediate level. Thought I still haven’t mastered taking my hands off the handlebars. However, this week’s weather has made me start to reevaluate my skill. At the start of the week, I had figured that the wind would probably blow through and for the time being I wouldn’t use my bike. Well after 3 days of waiting to bike it occurred to me that maybe the wind wasn’t going anywhere. So, reluctantly I tried to ride my bike to school despite the wind. I don’t know how often you go out and try to bike in 20-30 mph winds, but I’ll tell you it’s difficult. I find it even more incredible that no matter what direction I am traveling, the wind is always against me. It’s like mother nature has decided since I can sort of ride a bike its time to dial up the difficulty. The odd thing is the wind can be strong enough to blow people off their bikes, I’ve seen it happen. But, the Dutch aren’t even phased by this. These people look outside in the morning, see pouring rain and trees shaking from the wind, and think “looks like a great day for the bicycle.” I’m not convinced that they have a full understanding of the weather they live in. A normal human being would except that in a climate with so much rain and wind, a rain coat and boots might be appropriate. Wrong, the Dutch pretty much don’t wear these. Its like they get some sort of satisfaction from getting soaked. Regardless, my bike and I are still trying to figure out how to overcome these challenges, I’ll keep you posted on how it goes.

         Besides my biking endeavors, this week I decided to check out Rotterdam. My impetus for visiting here was twofold. First, I wanted to see a concert there for a small electronic artist. Second, I had heard that Rotterdam had some really cool modern architecture and I wanted to check it out. I was able to drag a friend along with me for the sightseeing, but couldn’t convince him to stay for the concert. Mainly because things start late here. Anyway, after a 10-minute train ride we arrived at the massive train station that is Rotterdam Central. This thing rivals an airport with the number of shops and restaurants it contains. One of the coolest parts was this building had a super interesting design, as seen in the picture above. After leaving the station, we were both struck by how the city is like a modern take on a classic Dutch town. For example, check out the picture of one of the cannels we came across. While there weren’t too many cannels here, all of them seemed to have this modern style to where the water and walkways are super close.

         A little bit more walking took us to what I think is called the Erasmus bridge. It’s your classic ultra-modern bridge and is one of the big picture points in the city. We walked across it hoping to find some cool things to do, but were sorely disappointed when we ended up in a fairly industrial area. Thus, we promptly walked right back across. As for the last part of our journey, we randomly walked into a restaurant whose menu appeared to be a blend of barbeque and Italian. The meal itself was kind of weird, as the blending of those two foods would be, but overall I enjoyed that it was unique. My buddy and I then hung out at a bar until about midnight when the doors to my concert finally opened. Unbelievably the concert started at 1:30am and ended at 6am, needless to say I only lasted until about 3am. I left the city tired and wet (because of course it was raining), yet excited to come back for another visit sometime soon. As for this coming week, I had originally thought I might go to Belgium for St. Patrick’s Day, but have since decided that I better finally go see Amsterdam and then Den Haag. Hopefully it will leave me with a lot to write about! Thanks as always for reading and I’ll see you again next week.
