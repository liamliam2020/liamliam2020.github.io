Two Cities in Two Days

Written: March 20, 2019   

         Welcome back to my continuing saga, this week I was successful in finally seeing Amsterdam. Going to Amsterdam has been a little bit of a sore subject for me. Originally, I had planned to go about a week after my initial arrival here with a tour led by a club at my school. Unfornatley, I stayed up way too late the night before and ended up sleeping through that one. Then, about two weeks later, a bunch of my friends decided we would all go and check the city out. I ended up having to work on an assignment with my lab partner pretty much the entire day, so I missed that one too. After these two close misses, it started to become a running joke with my friends that I was just never going to actually go. But, now I can say without a doubt that you shouldn’t skip Amsterdam. The city is basically a bigger and busier version of Delft. One of the things I really liked is that the city as a whole is really walkable. I think we spent about five hours there just wandering around the many canals and side streets. Dam square, picture featured above, was one of the many open areas we came across and easily one of the most photogenic.

         All the walking got me sufficiently hungry so we went out in search of food. My friends claimed that around the red-light district there were some excellent places to eat, so I skeptically went with it. I think they might have been right, as the Italian place we ended up in was incredible. Moreover, the bakery we stopped in for after dinner waffles was also delicious. Since we were all full, the next natural move was to head over to Rembrandt square to check out the night life. The bar we ended up finding was apparently one of the oldest hip-hop bars in world, an utter waste on me as I don’t listen to anything from that genre. I definitely liked Amsterdam and will be going back to check out more of the city’s sights.

         The following night was of course St. Patrick’s Day, so a few friends and I decided to go into The Hague to check out the festivities. Our only real goal was to find an Irish pub and then sit in that pub for however long felt right. Luckily for me, the first pub google maps could find was deep within the city so I got a change to see some of the Hague too. I’m ashamed to say that I was so busy enjoying my walk that I only snapped one photo. This is especially sad because the Hague is one of my favorite places I have been so far. I thought it had a bit more of a modern vibe going on, similar to Rotterdam, but there was still a lot of the older more interesting buildings as well. I also didn’t see a lot of canals, so the city was able to have a lot of winding roads and an interesting closed in feeling. The Hague is also the seat of government in the Netherlands so I was hoping I would just stumble across the parliament building, sadly I couldn’t be so lucky. I guess that means I’ll be making another trip here as well!

         To cap off this post, I feel its only right to tell you how my biking is going. With a very red face, I must inform you that I succeed in falling off my bike for the first time this week. I want to say that we all knew this was eventually going to happen. Thankfully, I wasn’t hurt and my bike seems to be alright as well. As for how this could have happened, I’m going to gracefully blame another biker. I was happily biking along, as I do, when I came up to a part of the road that has been under construction. So, the bike lane has this abrupt left turn in it and to make things worse both sides of traffic have to squeeze through this turn in a sort of a small space. Well one of the bikers in the other side of traffic (coming at me) decided he wanted to take up the entire lane. I slammed on my pedal brakes, but I was going too fast to stop, so I try and just squeeze through this turn anyway. Well I ended up clipping the side of a barrier, and promptly bouncing off of it. I didn’t actually fall off my bike, but I was certainly stopped dead in my tracks. I also opted to take a hot second to just stand there and access my body to make sure I wasn’t injured. Good times. I like to think that this has really helped me grow as a biker, if anything it has given me a healthy reality check. My goal for this week is to actually stay on my bike!
