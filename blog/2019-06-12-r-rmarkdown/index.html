Seeing the Dutch Country

Written: June 12, 2019   

         I had another wonderful week in the Netherlands. In my effort to see more of the country, I decided to visit Utrecht and Gouda. I did not have any set plans for either location, but I had read some blog posts to give myself an idea of what might be fun. First stop was Utrecht with two of my friends. The main thing we all noticed was how many people there were. This place was literally packed with crowds rivaling that of Amsterdam. The nice thing about a crowd is that you can just follow it from the train station to wherever the major attractions are. This led us to the Dom Tower. Unfornatley (and evermore a trend on my visits throughout Europe), the tower was going through some restorations so much of its beauty was obscured. We then moved on to follow the main canal in the city. This one is especially unique by Dutch standards because it’s sort of double decker. By that I mean, there is a main walkway above the canal set a little way back, then you can take stairs to go down right next to the canal. My picture demonstrates this more easily than words. Anyway, we walked along this canal for a while doing some window shopping and eventually selecting a spot to sit and have lunch. It was a really cute city to sit in and observe. For the rest of the afternoon, we just did some exploring mixed with sitting along the canal enjoy some beverages.

         The following day, I dragged a friend to see Gouda with me. I picked this place for really one reason and that is Gouda is where one of my favorite cheeses is produced. We got off the train and started to make our way into the city center when it occurred to us that not only was ever business closed, but also that the place was a ghost town. It made wandering the small side streets of the town really fun, but I was left with the feeling that not a lot happens in this town. Later on, while enjoying some lunch and chatting with our waiter, we learned that apparently it was a holiday so that’s why nobody was there. Honestly though, I think the town is just really not a big place. I think we walked around the entire place in less than an hour. Emptiness aside, they had a really cute town hall and an even cooler cheese house where they weight all the cheese in their weekly market. I’m really happy I visited but its not a place I would rush back to anytime soon and certainly not on a holiday.

         As an added bonus from that same holiday, I realized that I didn’t have any classes early in the week. So, I went in to the Hague on a rainy day to see one of the museums that has come highly recommended, the Mauritshuis. It has a really nice collection of Dutch masters like Rembrandt and Vermeer. While it was on the smaller side, it was very cool to visit and a great way to spent a rainy day.
