Academics

Written: May 8, 2019   

         Hello again, this week ended up being remarkably normal for me. I had planned originally planned to visit Haarlem, but after a cold and rainy storm system moved in, I opted to stay at home instead. Fear not, for I used this restful weekend to my advantage setting out the basic plans behind the rest of my travels while I’m here! I’ll be in Cologne Germany this weekend, then Luxembourg and Paris in the coming weeks, with even more to come after the semester. As hard as it is to believe my time here is starting to come to a close. It’s under two months left of school. As for my studies, I figured I would use this post to really talk about what I do when I’m not travelling. This quarter I have only three classes a robotic design class, a linear algebra class, and a business venturing class. The robotic design class has me grouped up with eight other students with the goal of designing a robot that can follow a black line on the group, while also avoiding obstacles in its path. Right now, we are still very much in the design phase, but so far the classes is really exciting. As for my linear algebra class, it is actually one of the last math classes I have to take. Once it’s done, all I will have left is statistics, which is crazy to me because when I first read the requirements for my degree, I didn’t know how I was going to finish so many math classes. I have also really been enjoying this math class unlike a lot of the calculus I had to take. What I really like about it is that one can see the direct crossovers into computer science and even some of my electrical engineering courses. Finally, my business venturing class I cannot say a lot about for two reasons. First, the way this classes transfers back to Pitt results in it not counting for any credits. This means I don’t really care about the class because it doesn’t do anything for me. I’m simply enrolled in it because TU Delft requires that I have a certain amount classes and I didn’t want to overload my schedule with difficult classes. This why it was really helpful for me to be taking full credit loads while I was at Pitt, because now I have some freedom in my schedule to enjoy my study abroad. Second, the class is at 8AM, so even though it looks interesting I often end up wanting sleep more than I want to sit in a lecture. I plan on going to a select few of the lectures because I can see from the class schedule that they will be covering some topics of interest to me. Otherwise, that class is mostly a write off for me. That’s what my studies are looking right as of now. I don’t really have any interesting photos to go along with this so hopefully this wall of text wasn’t too daunting.
