Welcome to My Blog

Written: January 29, 2019   

         Welcome to my very first blog post! I’m creating this as a way to hopefully share some of the experiences and travels that I have during my semester abroad. For those of you who might not know, my Spring 2019 semester will be spent at TU Delft in the Netherlands, instead of my typical Pittsburgh residence. The university is located about 35 minutes to the Southwest of Amsterdam, or 10 minutes outside of Rotterdam, in the small city of Delft. Originally the city’s claim to fame was a mix of their pottery manufacturing, known as Delft blue pottery, and breathtaking canals. However, it also holds a special place in fine art thanks to Vermeer who was born there in 1632.

         Studying abroad as an engineer can be tricky to pull off thanks to the number of credits we need to graduate and the fact that very few schools abroad meet the standards required by Pitt Engineering to count as transferable credits. That’s why Delft may seem like an odd choice compared to London, Sydney, or any other of the major cities people typically study in. In reality, this school and location were actually my top choice. The school itself is the most highly regarded university on the list that Pitt had pre-approved. Ranking #1 in the Netherlands, #6 in Europe, and #19 in the world for Engineering and Technology schools. Moreover, the location of the school, on continental Europe, is perfect for my needs. With Delft as my home base, I should be able to make use of the extensive train system in Europe to visit many of the major cities in the area.

        This leads into the meat of what this blog will be focusing on. I will be mainly writing about the countries and cities that I plan on visiting or about significant adventures I hope to have while in the Netherlands. I’ll do my best to capture pictures that you all might enjoy along the way. As of now, I don’t have any plans on how often these posts will appear, but I doubt I will be making more than one ever two weeks. I am also open to feedback, so please let me know if you have any ideas on how I can improve this blog. Moreover, this website was actually coded by myself, so if you encounter any bugs, broken links, or issues with the format I would be happy to fix/address them. Finally, my journey has not actually begun! I arrive in the Netherlands on the 5th of February and look forward to sharing more information with you all after I have settled down a little.
