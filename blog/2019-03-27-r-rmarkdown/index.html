Finding a Taste for Soccer

Written: March 27, 2019   

         Welcome back, this week was exciting for both me and this blog! A few people have been asking me to add a comment section to this blog and I finally got around to doing it. I’m confident in your powers of observation, but just in case the comment section is located at the bottom of each blog post and should work on most browsers. Although for whatever reason, I couldn’t post blog comments when I tried in Firefox, so if that’s your browser of choice I recommend Chrome, Safari, or even Edge. If you use Internet Explorer, do yourself a favor and switch to a new browser because I haven’t been adding compatibility for you (web developers across the world will thank you too). Moreover, I have been working on a complete theme redesign of this site too. I hope to have it done by the end of the week or maybe even by the time this blog post goes live! Please let me know what you think and more importantly if you find any bugs/broken links.

         As for my adventures this week I found myself exploring Amsterdam much more in depth. On Saturday, I decided to go into the city alone and just wander around for the afternoon. I was really excited to finally do this because last time I was there was with four other friends making aimlessly walking around not a viable option. Amsterdam is laid out in a half circle fashion with the canals cutting the city into smaller half circles. The major touristy part is in the center half circle, which is exclusively where I spent my time on my last trip. So, this time I ventured out into a further semi-circle and thus got to see a more residential part of the city. It was a peaceful experience and I was able to capture a lot of cool photos too. I also had a little bit of a sub-agenda too, which was trying to figure out how to effortlessly navigate the city on my feet.

         The following day, I was able to check a major European study abroad goal off my list: I went to a real soccer game! It was a EuroCup qualifier game between the Netherlands and Germany. I think I am correct in saying that it was my first professional soccer game too. As for the level of excitement around this game, I was utterly blown away by the fans. It all started when my friends and I entered into the metro station to get to the game. Right away, I heard loud chanting and started to see people dressed in bright orange (that’s the Netherlands home color). But when I say dressed in orange, I mean like literally people in bright orange suits, pants, hats, anything you can thing of. Its almost a little jarring because the only time I usually see that color is on emergency service personnel or stadium officials. All of this is only compounded by the fact that people can just legally drink in the subway, so you see people with their six-racks of beer singing pretty much acting like hooligans. Once we got into the metro, you could feel them shaking the train for the entire ride. As for the atmosphere in the actual stadium, it was next level. I think the only kind of American sporting event that creates the same energy is the Olympics because of their sense of national pride tied in with the match. Best of all, I actually got to see an exciting game. The only damper being that the Netherlands lost in the last minutes with a final score of 3-2. But, I’m really glad that I went to this game as it was one of those experiences I won’t soon forget.

         Now I won’t leave you without at least mentioning my biking too. So far, I have yet to run my bike into any obstacles this week. Additionally, as much as it pains me, I really do see the utility of riding my bike as it has become my major form of transportation. I just wish I could ride along without having to use my hands, it almost looks fun! I see the Dutch kids chugging around just texting with both hands off the handle bars, as if they are mocking me. I guess that means I need more practice. Also, on a sperate note, some of you may be wondering how I am doing with a task my Dad gave me. The task being to find a girl in wooden shoes and to get a photo with her, preferably with a windmill and tulips in the background. I will have you know that contrary to popular belief these girls don’t grow on trees. I have been searching high and low for over a month now with absolutely nothing to show for it. I figured that at least in Amsterdam I could find somebody posing for tourist, but no such luck. I’ll keep looking, but my hopes are starting to get dashed. I don’t have any plans for next week as of yet, but I’m sure I’ll think if something in the meantime. See you next week!
