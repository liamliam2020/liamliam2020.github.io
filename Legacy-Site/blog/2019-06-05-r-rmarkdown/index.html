A Real Vacation

Written: June 5, 2019   

         Hello again, this week I had the pleasure of visiting Paris. I departed by train and was really impressed at how close Paris is to me, only two and a half hours away. Upon my arrival, I thought I would simply walk over to my hostel. I really under estimated how large Paris is as it took me nearly an hour to walk all that way. With little daylight left, I dropped my bags and quickly walked over to the Eiffel Tower to enjoy the end of the day. Luckily for me, my hostel was just about a ten-minute walk from the sight. I sat on a bench and watched the tower for some time until hunger eventually forced me to search out dinner. I ate a quick meal and walked back to my hostel to get some sleep. I was super lucky because for whatever reason the hostel I had selected was especially nice. The beds were comfy and the room was practically empty so I felt like I was staying in a real hotel for a change.

         The next morning, I set out for Monet Museum housing, I think, the largest collection of Monet pieces in all of Paris. But before I could handle all that artwork, I needed to fuel up with breakfast. I went to a place called HolyBelly, which came heavily recommended by my parents. I got there within twenty-minutes of its opening and found myself waiting in a line for another fifteen-minutes. In this time, I got to wondering how good the food could be, but mostly flabbergasted at the number of Americans waiting in line with me. Once I finally got a table, I ordered a large stack of pancakes that were covered in bourbon butter, fired eggs, hash browns, and crispy bacon. It was utterly delicious. The bottom of the receipt said that they hoped to see me again tomorrow and I was in total agreement. Next, I took the metro to the museum and prepared myself for what I expected was going to be an impressive showing. I spent roughly an hour and a half there just enjoying the assortment of Monet works that they had. Additionally, the museum itself was really cool to see because they had designed the space specially to show the pieces in it. I think I learned that the way art is displayed is an artform in and of itself. Afterwards, I spent some time enjoying a pleasant park next to the museum before taking the metro back into the heart of the city. I found myself on a train towards the Louvre so I figured I might as well just go there and walk around the outside for a bit. I had forgotten just how large the park outside of that museum is, so I really spent some time just getting lost. Eventually I happened across some lounging chairs and proceeded to spend the better part of the afternoon just sitting and enjoying the day. I should mention that the weather the entire time I was in Paris was absolutely excellent, maybe even too much as I ended up getting a bit of a sunburn. My next order of business was to go see Notre Dame since I was really curious as to the extent of the damage to the landmark. Sadly, it was really hard to see anything because there were large metal walls set up all around the base of the church. Moreover, the part where the roof had collapsed was covered in scaffolding, obstructing my view more. As best as I could tell, the main towers of church looked unmarked while the roof looked in shambles. For sure a sad sight to see, thought I found it really interesting to see the church in its burned-out state. By this time, I had used up most of the day so I opted to grab another quick dinner and head to bed.

         For my final day in the city, I really wanted to visit the Pompidou Center where all the modern art is held. But to start, I had to get another of HolyBelly’s excellent breakfast. I was happy to find that this time there was no wait and I was able to quickly order my food. I decided to really go all in today, so I ordered scrambled eggs, toast, hash browns, sausage, pear juice, and afterwards a piece of chocolate brownie cake. It was really yummy. I just wish I could have eaten at this place even more than I did. I then popped over to the Pompidou and waited in line for them to open the museum. I think I spent about two and a half hours here maybe even a full three just wandering around their many exhibits and enjoying one of the best modern art museums I think I’ve ever been to. I wouldn’t say I’m the biggest fan of most modern art, but I am really a sucker for some of abstract works (think Jackson Pollock) and more so for the exhibits where you can interact with the works. I only had a little bit more time left before I had to grab my train, so I went to the Place des Vosges to sit and read my book. I really enjoyed my time here and was sad when I eventually had to leave to go find my train. This trip really felt like my first vacation, as I was able to sit back and relax in a wonderful place. Now I am back in Delft and starting to prepare myself for my final exams. Its hard to believe that my time here is nearly over, but I’m hoping to make the most of my last few weeks of school by really exploring some of the spots in the Netherlands I have neglected. See you next week!
